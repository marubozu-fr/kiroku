▾ How this projection works

The fan is a Monte Carlo simulation — it does not draw straight lines into the future. Instead it replays your own trading edge thousands of times and shows the range of plausible outcomes.

  1. Sample your history. Every closed trade in the filtered window contributes its R-multiple (profit/loss measured in units of risk). Here that is 120 trades with expectancy +0.28R and a per-trade standard deviation of 1.95R.
  2. Estimate remaining trades. Your historical pace (~10 trades / month) projects ~65 more trades from now until 31 Dec.
  3. Run N simulations. For each of 10,000 simulated years we draw that many R-multiples at random, with replacement from your real distribution and accumulate them on top of today's +18.4R.
  4. Derive percentile bands. At each month-end we read the 10th, 25th, 50th, 75th and 90th percentiles across all simulations. The median is P50; the shaded P25–P75 and P10–P90 bands are the confidence intervals. They widen over time because uncertainty compounds with each additional trade.
  5. Read the answers. Goal probability is the share of simulations that finish at or above your goal (41% reach +40R). Risk of ruin is the share that finish negative (1.0%).

This is a model of your past behaviour, not a promise. It assumes future trades resemble past ones and ignores changes in market regime, position sizing or discipline.
